INTRODUCTION:

There are many kinds of storms that draw their power from the wind. Tropical storms are among them. These storms, including hurricanes, are often associated with the damage caused by the waves they create. Hurricanes are wind-based funnel storms that form with low pressure and create storm clouds by picking up moisture from the ocean1. We decided to investigate the extreme winds of tropical storms from 2000-2015 using NOAA hurricane data2we found on Kaggle.

# Load Packages
library(tidyverse)
library(lubridate)
library(stringr)
library(tidyselect)
library(leaflet)

# Load Data
pacific <- read.csv("pacific.csv")
atlantic <- read.csv("atlantic.csv")
# Clean Variables of Interest
# Change Name to a character and Max. Wind to a number
atlantic <- atlantic %>%
  mutate(Name=as.character(Name))

# Change Name to a character and Max. Wind to a number
pacific<- pacific %>%
  mutate(Name=as.character(Name))

# Join the datasets for Hurricane
hurricane <- pacific %>%
  full_join(atlantic, by= c("Name", "Date", "Maximum.Wind")) %>%
  select(Name, Date, Maximum.Wind)

# Filter to include dates between 2000-2015 
# Formate Date & Create Event
hurricane2<- hurricane %>%
  filter( 20160101 >= Date & Date> 20000101)%>%
  mutate(Date=ymd(Date), Event= "Tropical Cyclone")

# Group Tropical Storms by name, and find average maximum wind speed in descending order
hurricane3 <- hurricane2 %>%
  group_by(Name) %>%
  summarize(MW = mean(Maximum.Wind))%>%
  arrange(desc(MW))%>%
  slice(1:15)

Are There Trends in Tropical Storms’ Average Maximum Wind Speeds?

The chart above, shows the tropical storms’ averaged maximum wind speed using the atlantic and pacific datasets3 from NOAA. Looking at the tropical storm data globally over the 15-year period we made some observations which can be explained by previously known facts about tropical storms. For instance, the chart made with the datasets 4 from NOAA, shows a fairly consistent pattern of plumes separated by gaps of time between observations. These gaps can be explained by the fact that tropical cyclones only occur from June to November5. One may note that in 2013 the range of average maximum wind speed was much smaller than the surrounding years; this could be attributed to the fact the Pacific and Atlantic hurricane seasons were both shorter than average in 20136. Another year of interest is 2015 because of its two storms with ridiculously high wind speeds, which can be noted on the chart made using NOAA’s dataset7. Hurricanes Danny and Joaquin were both huge category four hurricanes in the Atlantic that year; Danny is a notable storm because it only lasted for a few days before dissipating whereas Hurricane Joaquin spent nearly a month sweeping up the gulf coast8.
